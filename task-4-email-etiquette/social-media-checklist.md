# Social Media Checklist for College Students

## 5 Do's

- [ ] Think before you post — Ask yourself if you'd be comfortable with a future employer, professor, or family member seeing it.
- [ ] Use privacy settings — Regularly review and update who can see your posts, photos, and personal information.
- [ ] Build a positive digital footprint — Share your achievements, projects, volunteer work, and interests that reflect your values.
- [ ] Be respectful and constructive — Engage in discussions with kindness and professionalism, even when you disagree.
- [ ] Credit and support others — Always tag, mention, or acknowledge people whose work or ideas you share or reference.

## 5 Don'ts

- [ ] Don't share personal details publicly — Never post your home address, phone number, real-time location, or daily schedule.
- [ ] Don't post impulsively — Avoid sharing content when you're emotional, upset, or under the influence — it's hard to take back.
- [ ] Don't spread misinformation — Always verify news and information before sharing; check the original source.
- [ ] Don't overshare personal conflicts — Venting about classmates, professors, or employers online can seriously damage your reputation.
- [ ] Don't engage with trolls or cyberbullies — Block, report, and disengage rather than escalating the situation.
